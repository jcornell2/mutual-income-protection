Why Own-Occupation Disability Matters

Your medical training is your most valuable asset. Group coverage at the hospital often isn't enough.

True Own-Occupation Protection

If illness or injury stops you from performing your specialty, benefits pay — even if you can work in another role. Group policies often switch to "any occupation" after 24 months.

Future Income Option (FIO)

Lock in the right to increase coverage as your attending income grows — without new medical exams. Critical for residents and fellows whose income will jump after training.

Ask me how FIO fits your training timeline.

Cost of Living Adjustment (COLA)

If you're on claim, benefits can increase annually to help keep pace with inflation — protecting real purchasing power over a long career.

Extended Partial Disability

Covers income loss when you return part-time in your specialty — not just total disability. Especially valuable for surgeons and proceduralists.
